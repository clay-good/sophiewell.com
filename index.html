Why Sophie Well

Why your data never leaves the browser

Sophie Well is a static website. Every calculation, decoder, and lookup runs in JavaScript on your device. A strict Content Security Policy blocks third-party scripts, analytics, and outbound network calls at the browser level. Nothing you type is uploaded - because there is no server to upload it to.

Why these numbers are trustworthy

Every formula is implemented from its primary citation and covered by unit tests against the published worked example. Every code lookup is sourced from a public dataset (CMS, FDA, NLM, NUBC, X12) and stamped with the fetched-on date. The full source is on GitHub: you can read, fork, and verify it line by line.

Why this is free

Sophie Well is a personal project shipped as a public utility under the MIT license. There is no paywall, no account, no upsell, and no ad inventory. The cost of running it is the cost of static hosting, which the author covers because that is the right shape for a reference tool that anyone might need at 11 p.m.

Frequently asked

Is Sophie Well free?

Yes. Sophie Well is free, open source under the MIT license, and ad-free. The full source is on GitHub.

Is Sophie Well safe to use with patient data?

Every calculation runs locally in your browser. Nothing you type is sent to any server. The site enforces a strict Content Security Policy and ships no analytics, telemetry, or third-party CDN. You alone control whether information stays on your device.

Does Sophie Well work offline?

Yes, after first load. A service worker caches the static bundle so every tool keeps working without an internet connection.

Does Sophie Well replace my EHR calculator?

No. Sophie Well is a deterministic reference aid. Your EHR remains the system of record for documentation, orders, and billing. Use Sophie Well as a check, not a replacement.

Where does Sophie Well's data come from?

Public datasets (CMS, FDA, OIG, AHA, CDC, ATSDR, NIOSH, DOT) plus published clinical literature. Each tool shows its primary citation and, where applicable, a dataset Source label with the fetched-on date in the References region under the result.

Can I use Sophie Well for billing decisions?

Sophie Well is a reference and educational tool. Use it to look up codes, decode statements, and learn the workflow; payer-specific rules, contracted rates, and certified billing review govern actual claims.

Is Sophie Well HIPAA compliant?

HIPAA applies to covered entities and their business associates. Sophie Well is neither: it is a static website that stores no data and transmits nothing you type. Whether you may use it with PHI depends on your organization's policies, not on a vendor BAA.

Does Sophie Well use AI?

No. Every tool is a pure deterministic function or a lookup against a bundled public dataset. No models, no inference, no LLM calls, in the browser or anywhere else.

How current are the codes (ICD-10, HCPCS, CPT)?

ICD-10-CM follows the annual fiscal-year update (October). HCPCS is refreshed quarterly. CPT descriptors are AMA-owned and are not bundled; structural CPT rows come from the CMS Medicare Physician Fee Schedule. Each tool surfaces its dataset fetched-on date.

Can I trust Sophie Well's clinical scores?

Every clinical formula is implemented from the primary citation shown in its References region, and is covered by unit tests against the published example values. Sophie Well is a check on the math, not a substitute for clinical judgment.

How do I report a bug or wrong answer?

Open an issue at github.com/clay-good/sophiewell.com/issues. Include the tile, the inputs, the expected output, and the citation you compared against. Bug fixes ship as small PRs against main.

Who built Sophie Well?

Clay Good builds and maintains Sophie Well. It is a personal project shipped as a public utility under the MIT license.
