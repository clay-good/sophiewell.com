> [spec-v77](spec-v77.md) billing & coding program. Adds **5 deterministic

## 1. Thesis

The Medicare Physician Fee Schedule allowable for one line is not a lookup — it is
a computation the biller redoes for every claim:

```
allowed = [ workRVU·workGPCI + peRVU·peGPCI + mpRVU·mpGPCI ] × CF
```

…and then it is **reduced**, in a fixed order, by the policies the line's
modifiers and the code's MPFS indicators trigger: multiple-procedure (mod 51),
bilateral (mod 50), assistant/co-/team surgeon (mod 80/81/82/AS/62/66), and
finally the 2% sequestration cut on the Medicare-paid portion. Get the order or
an indicator wrong and the posted payment is wrong — silently. No public tool puts
the whole chain on a phone with the policy indicators wired in. v78 does.

The engine **reads** the bundled `data/mpfs` corpus already on disk (work/PE/MP
RVUs, global period, status code) and the new `data/gpci` triplets, and accepts a
hand-entered RVU/GPCI/CF for any code or locality not in the bundle (doctrine
clause 2 — the tool never fails for lack of a row). The conversion factor and the
fixed reduction percentages are **dated, ledger-tracked constants** (ruleFamily
`billing-v78`); the user can override the CF to model a non-Medicare contract
priced as a percentage of the fee schedule.

## 2. The five tiles

Each passes the [spec-v29](spec-v29.md) §3 one-line test, computes money in
integer cents, ships its primary citation inline with a [spec-v60](spec-v60.md)
`accessed` + ledger row, and renders the [spec-v77](spec-v77.md) §2 "rule's math,
not a payment guarantee" note. Near-neighbors named; none duplicates an existing
tile.

### 2.1 `rvu-payment` — MPFS allowed amount (the locality-priced line)
- **Citation:** 42 CFR §414.20–§414.22 (RVU components and GPCI adjustment); SSA
  §1848(b); the annual PFS Final Rule conversion factor; CMS PFS Relative Value
  Files. CMS MLN *Medicare Physician Fee Schedule* booklet.
- **citationUrl:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-42/part-414/subpart-B
- **Group:** Billing & Reimbursement (`B`). **Audiences:** `billers`, `coders`,
  `practice-managers`.
- **Inputs:** CPT/HCPCS code (looks up work/PE/MP RVUs and the **facility vs
  non-facility** PE from `data/mpfs`, or the three RVUs entered by hand); Medicare
  locality (looks up the GPCI triplet from `data/gpci`, or entered by hand); place
  of service / site flag (selects facility vs non-facility PE RVU); conversion
  factor (preset = current dated CMS CF; override to model a % -of-Medicare
  contract); optional units.
- **Output:** the **non-facility and facility allowed amounts**, the GPCI-adjusted
  RVU subtotals shown as a [spec-v48](spec-v48.md) derivation (`work·gpci`,
  `pe·gpci`, `mp·gpci`, total RVU, × CF), and the **site-of-service differential**
  (the dollars lost or gained moving the service into a facility). This is the
  anchor every other v78 tile reduces from. **Near-neighbor:** the v83
  `apc-payment`/`drg-payment` compute the *facility's* payment for the same
  encounter; this computes the *professional* fee.

### 2.2 `mppr` — Multiple-procedure payment reduction (the 100/50/50 chain)
- **Citation:** CMS Pub. 100-04 Claims Processing Manual, Ch. 12 §40.6 (multiple
  surgery: 100% highest, 50% each subsequent), §40.7 (endoscopy base-code rule),
  and the diagnostic-imaging (TC) and therapy (PE-component) MPPR provisions
  (Ch. 12 §90 / §20). MPFS `MULT PROC` indicator (0/2/3/4/9).
- **citationUrl:** https://www.cms.gov/regulations-and-guidance/guidance/manuals/downloads/clm104c12.pdf
- **Group:** Billing & Reimbursement (`B`). **Audiences:** `billers`, `coders`,
  `surgical-billing`.
- **Inputs:** a list of lines (each: code or fee, and its `MULT PROC` indicator —
  looked up from `data/mpfs` or entered); reduction category (**surgical 100/50/50**,
  **endoscopy** base-value, **imaging TC**, or **therapy PE**); the per-category
  reduction percentage (preset from the cited manual, overridable).
- **Output:** the lines **re-ranked by fee** with the reduction applied per the
  selected policy (full pay on the primary, the cited percentage on each
  subsequent; endoscopy subtracts the base-procedure value before reducing), the
  **per-line and total expected allowed**, and the **dollars withheld by the
  reduction**. The single most common cause of "we expected $X, got $Y" on a
  multi-line surgical claim. **Near-neighbor:** `bilateral-pay` (a *single* line at
  150%); compose them by feeding a bilateral line's adjusted fee into the ranking.

### 2.3 `bilateral-pay` — Modifier 50 bilateral payment (by MPFS indicator)
- **Citation:** CMS Pub. 100-04, Ch. 12 §40.7; MPFS **`BILAT SURG`** indicator
  (0 = not allowed; 1 = 150% of fee for the pair; 2 = already priced as bilateral,
  100%; 3 = pay each side at full, no reduction; 9 = concept doesn't apply).
- **citationUrl:** https://www.cms.gov/medicare/payment/fee-schedules/physician
- **Group:** Billing & Reimbursement (`B`). **Audiences:** `billers`, `coders`.
- **Inputs:** the line's base fee or code (fee via `rvu-payment`/`data/mpfs`); the
  code's bilateral indicator (looked up or entered).
- **Output:** the **payable amount for the bilateral service** under the indicator
  (150% at indicator 1; 100% at 2; 200% at 3; a hard "modifier 50 not payable —
  bill differently" at 0/9), with the indicator and rule cited. Stops the two most
  common bilateral errors at once: billing 50 on an indicator-0 code, and
  expecting 200% where the code is already priced bilaterally.

### 2.4 `multi-surgeon-pay` — Assistant / co-surgeon / team-surgeon payment
- **Citation:** CMS Pub. 100-04, Ch. 12 §20.4.3, §40.8; MPFS `ASST SURG`,
  `CO SURG`, `TEAM SURG` indicators. Payment percentages: assistant at surgery
  (mod 80/81/82/AS) = **16% of the fee**; co-surgeon (mod 62) = **62.5% to each**;
  team surgeon (mod 66) = by-report/carrier-priced.
- **citationUrl:** https://www.cms.gov/regulations-and-guidance/guidance/manuals/downloads/clm104c12.pdf
- **Group:** Billing & Reimbursement (`B`). **Audiences:** `billers`, `coders`,
  `surgical-billing`.
- **Inputs:** the primary-surgeon fee or code; the role (assistant / PA-or-NP
  assistant via AS / co-surgeon / team surgeon); the code's matching surgical
  indicator (looked up or entered — the gate on whether the role is **payable at
  all** for this code).
- **Output:** the **allowed amount for the assisting/co-/team surgeon** (16% for an
  assistant — with the AS-modifier non-physician note; 62.5% per co-surgeon; "by
  report — indicator requires documentation" for team), plus a hard
  **"indicator = 0/9: this role is not separately payable for this code"** gate so
  the user never bills an assistant on a code that disallows one. **Near-neighbor:**
  `rvu-payment` supplies the base fee this percentage is taken from.

### 2.5 `sequestration-adjust` — The 2% Medicare sequestration cut
- **Citation:** Budget Control Act of 2011 (Pub. L. 112-25), §251A; the Medicare
  **2% payment reduction** applied to the Medicare-paid amount **after** deductible
  and coinsurance. CMS MLN sequestration guidance.
- **citationUrl:** https://www.cms.gov/medicare/payment
- **Group:** Billing & Reimbursement (`B`). **Audiences:** `billers`,
  `practice-managers`.
- **Inputs:** the Medicare-approved (allowed) amount for the line/claim; the
  patient's deductible-and-coinsurance portion; the sequestration percentage
  (preset 2%, a dated ledger-tracked constant — overridable to model the suspended/
  phased rates Congress has set in the past).
- **Output:** the **80%-payable base**, the **sequestration dollars withheld**
  (percentage applied to the program-payment portion, **not** to the
  beneficiary's coinsurance), and the **net Medicare check**. The last reduction in
  the chain; chains directly off `rvu-payment` → `mppr` output. Renders the
  explicit note that sequestration reduces the **payment**, never the patient's
  cost-share or the allowed amount.

## 3. Robustness

- **Money is integer cents end-to-end.** Every compute function works in cents and
  formats once at the render edge; no float `toFixed` leak (the
  [spec-v62](spec-v62.md) session-5 NaN-leak class is closed by construction).
  Zero `NaN`/`Infinity` from the [spec-v59](spec-v59.md) fuzz harness is a merge
  gate; negative, zero, and missing-RVU inputs fall back to a `note`, never a
  wrong number.
- **Reduction order is fixed and tested.** The canonical order — base allowed →
  bilateral → multiple-procedure ranking → assistant/co/team percentage →
  sequestration — is encoded once and each tile states where in the chain it sits,
  so composing them (output of one into the next) is well-defined and unit-tested
  with a worked four-line surgical example.
- **Indicators gate, they don't guess.** Every indicator-0/9 path is a hard
  "not payable / does not apply" message, never a silent $0 or a wrong percentage.
- **Dated constants are ledger-tracked.** The PFS conversion factor, the 2%
  sequestration rate, the 16%/62.5% surgical percentages, and the 100/50/50 and
  150% reduction factors each get a `pa-staleness-ledger.json` row
  (ruleFamily `billing-v78`) with `lastVerified`; `check-pa-staleness` fails the
  build when any goes stale past the policy window. The CF override field lets a
  user model a contract without waiting for a bundle refresh.

## 5. Acceptance criteria

- All 5 tiles in §2 are live in Group B: each has a `META[id]` entry, an inline
  cited output with `citationUrl` + `accessed`, ≥3 boundary worked examples in its
  unit test, a [spec-v11](spec-v11.md) audit log, and passes [spec-v29](spec-v29.md)
  §3.
- `rvu-payment` reproduces a hand-checked CMS example (a known code + locality + CF
  → published allowed, facility and non-facility) to the cent; the site-of-service
  differential equals the PE-RVU delta × peGPCI × CF.
- `mppr` reproduces the 100/50/50 surgical example and the endoscopy base-rule
  example; `bilateral-pay` returns 150/100/200%/not-payable for indicators
  1/2/3/0; `multi-surgeon-pay` returns 16%/62.5%/by-report and the not-payable gate
  for the disallowing indicator.
- Every dated constant has a `pa-staleness-ledger.json` row (ruleFamily
  `billing-v78`) and an `accessed` date; `check-pa-staleness` passes.
- `data/gpci` loads, and a hand-entered GPCI/RVU/CF override makes every tile work
  for a code/locality absent from the bundle (doctrine clause 2 verified).
- `lib/billing-v78.js` is in the [spec-v59](spec-v59.md) fuzz harness with zero
  non-finite / `Invalid Date` leaks; all money is integer cents.
- `UTILITIES.length` is **342** and all catalog-truth surfaces

## 6. Out of scope for v78

- **No bundled NCCI/MUE edits** (that is [spec-v79](spec-v79.md)); v78 prices a
  line, it does not decide whether the line survives an edit.
- **No facility payment** (DRG/APC are [spec-v83](spec-v83.md)); v78 is the
  professional fee only.
- **No anesthesia or therapy time-unit pricing** (that is [spec-v80](spec-v80.md)):
  those use a base-unit/time formula, not the RVU formula.
- **No browsable fee-schedule or locality directory.** The tool computes from the
  user's code + locality + CF; it ships no searchable national fee table beyond the
  public-domain RVU/GPCI values used to compute, per [spec-v77](spec-v77.md) §8.
