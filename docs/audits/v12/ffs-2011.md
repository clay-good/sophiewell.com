- Auditor: CG

- Citation re-verified against: Guillevin L, Pagnoux C, Seror R, et al. The Five-Factor Score revisited. Medicine (Baltimore). 2011;90(1):19-27 (cross-verified against the PubMed primary abstract, PMID 21200183, and a second independent reproduction of the factors and mortality bands).

`lib/rheum-v148.js ffs2011()` consumes the four poor-prognosis factors plus the
favorable absence-of-ENT factor and computes the FFS total 0-5 with the published
5-year-mortality band. Class A.

## Source-governance notes
- Four poor-prognosis factors each +1: age > 65, cardiac insufficiency, GI
  involvement, renal insufficiency (stabilized peak creatinine >= 150 umol/L ~ 1.7
  mg/dL). The 2011 revision DROPPED proteinuria and hematuria, keeping only the
  creatinine-based renal criterion.
- Favorable factor: ABSENCE of ENT manifestations scores +1 (ENT involvement
  signals a better prognosis in GPA/EGPA). Sign and applicability confirmed.
- 5-year mortality: ~9% at FFS 0, ~21% at FFS 1, ~40% at FFS >= 2.

## Boundary worked examples added
- FFS 0 -> 9%; FFS 1 -> 21%; FFS 2 -> 40% (the 1->2 band change); all-five 5/5;
  absence-of-ENT alone counts as a factor.

## Edge-input handling notes
- Five boolean flags summed; no division. num() bounds the 0-5 total. Covered by
  the spec-v59 fuzz harness, zero leaks.
